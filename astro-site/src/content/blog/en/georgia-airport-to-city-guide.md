## Your Arrival Options

Tbilisi International Airport (IATA: TBS) serves direct routes from Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Kuwait, Riyadh (via connections), and Doha. Here's how to get to your accommodation after landing.

## Option 1: Private Pre-Booked Transfer (Recommended for Families)

**Cost:** USD 20–35 one-way  
**Time:** 25–40 minutes  
**Luggage:** Any amount

A Georgia Hills driver meets you at the arrivals exit holding a name sign. This option eliminates language barriers, currency confusion, and the exhaustion of navigating public transport after a long flight. For families with children or luggage, this is the clear choice.

## Option 2: Official Airport Taxi

**Cost:** GEL 40–60 (USD 15–22) metered  
**Time:** 25–40 minutes

Taxis operate from the designated rank at Terminal 1 and Terminal 2 exits. Meters are mandatory. Do not accept rides from unlicensed drivers who solicit inside the terminal.

## Option 3: Tbilisi Metro

**Cost:** GEL 1 (under USD 0.40)  
**Time:** 35–50 minutes (with change at Isani)

The airport metro station (Airport Line) connects to Isani on the Akhmeteli-Varketili line. Change at Isani for Rustaveli or Liberty Square (city centre). Practical for solo travelers with one carry-on, challenging for families.

## Option 4: Ridesharing Apps

**Apps:** Bolt, Yandex Taxi  
**Cost:** GEL 25–45 (USD 9–17)  
**Time:** 25–40 minutes

Both apps work reliably in Tbilisi and connect to credit cards. Useful if your pre-booked driver is delayed. Arabic language is not supported in-app; English interface is available.

## Late-Night Arrivals

Many Gulf flights arrive between 23:00 and 03:00. The metro does not run after 00:30. Plan for a taxi or private transfer for late arrivals. Georgia Hills can arrange 24-hour pickup.

## Luggage and Currency Tips

- ATMs are available in both terminal buildings. Georgian Lari is the local currency.
- No currency exchange needed at the airport — better rates are available in city centre exchange offices.
- Trolley carts are free in the baggage hall.
