## Overview

A week in Georgia gives you the perfect balance of city culture, mountain scenery, and coastal relaxation. This itinerary is built around the needs of GCC travelers — halal-conscious dining, prayer timing, and family pacing.

## Days 1–3: Tbilisi

Arrive and settle in the Vera or Saburtalo neighborhoods, which offer the best access to halal restaurants and convenience stores.

- **Day 1:** Old Town walking tour — Narikala Fortress, Abanotubani sulfur baths district, Metekhi Church viewpoint. Evening at Rustaveli Avenue.
- **Day 2:** Mtatsminda Park (cable car), National Museum, and the weekend Dry Bridge Market for handicrafts.
- **Day 3:** Day trip to Mtskheta (ancient capital, 30 minutes from Tbilisi) and Jvari Monastery. Back for a relaxed dinner.

## Day 4: Kazbegi

An early departure ensures you arrive at the Gergeti Trinity Church viewpoint before midday crowds. The drive through the Georgian Military Highway passes the Gudauri ski resort area.

- Stop at the Russian Arch at Zhinvali Reservoir for photos.
- 4WD vehicle recommended for the Gergeti ascent.
- Overnight in Stepantsminda — clear mountain air and star-filled skies.

## Day 5: Kazbegi to Batumi

Return drive to Tbilisi, collect luggage, then onward to Batumi (4.5 hours). The Black Sea coastal approach at sunset is memorable.

## Days 6–7: Batumi

- **Day 6:** Batumi Boulevard, Alphabet Tower, Europe Square, and Ali & Nino statue. Evening seafood dinner.
- **Day 7:** Botanical Garden (9 km from city centre), optional cable car to Anuria. Departure transfer to Batumi International Airport.

## Practical Tips

- Book accommodation in Tbilisi near Vake or Didube for prayer facility access.
- Carry a digital prayer timetable — Georgia uses UTC+4 year-round.
- Private transfers are 30–40% cheaper than metered taxis for airport runs.
