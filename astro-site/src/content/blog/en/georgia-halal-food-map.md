## Tbilisi: Halal Dining Districts

### Old Town (Abanotubani & Metekhi)
The Old Town is the most Arab-friendly area of Tbilisi. Look for restaurants displaying Arabic menus — these are typically halal-certified or halal-managed.

**Recommended areas:**
- Leselidze Street: Multiple cafes and grills with Arabic menus
- Gorgasali Square: Family-style Georgian-Arabic fusion restaurants
- Nariman Narimanov Street: Budget-friendly halal fast food

### Modern Tbilisi (Rustaveli & Vake)
Major shopping centre food courts offer halal fast food chains familiar to Gulf visitors. Vake district has several upscale halal restaurants with English and Arabic menus.

## Batumi: Halal Dining

Batumi's tourism-heavy economy means halal food is widely available near the Boulevard area. Turkish and Arabic restaurants cluster around Ninoshvili Street and Chavchavadze Street.

**Key tip:** Batumi's Orbi City complex includes a food court with halal-certified options.

## Prayer Facilities

### Tbilisi
- **Jumah Mosque**: Old Town, open for all five prayers. Wudu facilities available.
- **Al-Nour Prayer Room**: Didube district, near the central bus station.
- Several hotels in tourist areas provide prayer mats and Qibla direction on request.

### Kazbegi
Friday prayer options are limited; plan for independent prayer with a travel mat. The mountains make for a spiritually memorable place to pray.

### Batumi
- **Batumi Mosque**: Near the city centre, open daily.
- Some hotels near the seafront provide prayer facilities.

## Practical Tips

- Download the "Muslim Pro" or "Athan" app for accurate Tbilisi prayer times (UTC+4).
- Georgia does not adjust clocks; the same timezone applies year-round.
