## Why Winter Works for Gulf Families

For families from Saudi Arabia, UAE, or Kuwait, winter Georgia is a unique experience: proper snow, mountain skiing, and a dramatic temperature contrast from the Gulf heat. Georgia's November–February season offers genuine winter scenery without extreme cold.

## Gudauri Ski Resort

**Elevation:** 2,196m – 3,279m  
**Distance from Tbilisi:** 120km (approximately 2 hours)  
**Season:** December – April  
**Difficulty:** Beginner through Expert slopes available

Gudauri is the Caucasus's most accessible international ski resort. Direct transfers from Tbilisi operate daily during peak season. Rental equipment, English/Arabic-speaking instructors, and halal restaurant options are available.

**Family ski package estimate:**
- Full ski gear rental per person: GEL 60–90/day (USD 22–33)
- Group lesson (2 hours): GEL 80–120/person
- Resort day pass: GEL 75–100/person

## Tbilisi Winter Activities

- **Christmas Market at Freedom Square**: December through early January — lights, local crafts, mulled Georgian wine (non-alcoholic grape juice alternatives available).
- **Georgian National Museum Winter Season**: Uncrowded, excellent rotating exhibits.
- **Traditional Baths**: Abanotubani's sulfur baths are particularly enjoyable in cold weather — private rooms avoid mixed bathing.
- **Georgian Feast (Supra)**: Winter is the peak season for traditional supra-style restaurant experiences.

## Practical Packing for Georgia Winter

- Heavy coat, hat, and gloves for Tbilisi (can drop to -5°C in January).
- Waterproof snow boots for mountain visits.
- Layers — indoor heating in Georgian restaurants and hotels is excellent.

## Off-Season Savings

Winter travel (excluding Christmas/New Year week) saves significantly:
- Accommodation: 40–50% lower than summer rates
- Flights: 20–35% lower on Gulf–Tbilisi routes
- Private driver: Same rate year-round — no seasonal surcharge with Georgia Hills

A family of 4 can visit Georgia in January for approximately USD 2,200–3,500 all-in.
