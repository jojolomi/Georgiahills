## Why Georgia Offers Strong Value

The Georgian Lari (GEL) exchange rate favors Gulf currencies. As of 2026, 1 USD ≈ 2.70 GEL, and direct flights from Riyadh, Dubai, and Kuwait operate year-round.

## Private Driver Daily Rates

- **Sedan — city coverage:** USD 80–100/day
- **Sedan — mountain/long route (Kazbegi):** USD 100–130/day
- **Minivan (6–7 seats):** USD 120–160/day
- **Airport transfer (one-way):** USD 25–40

Rates include fuel. Accommodation for overnight trips is separate (typically GEL 40–80/night for driver).

## Accommodation Price Guide

- **Budget guesthouse:** USD 35–65/night
- **Mid-range 3-star hotel:** USD 70–130/night
- **Premium 4-star hotel:** USD 150–250/night
- **Luxury boutique (Tbilisi Old Town):** USD 280–500/night

Batumi rates are 20–30% higher during July–August peak season. Book 4–6 weeks ahead for summer travel.

## Dining Costs

- Family dinner at halal restaurant: GEL 80–150 (USD 30–55)
- Casual lunch per person: GEL 20–35 (USD 8–13)
- Coffee/specialty drink: GEL 6–10 (USD 2.50–4)
- Supermarket (daily essentials): GEL 20–40 for a family

## Activity Costs

- Gergeti Trinity Church 4WD trip: USD 40–70
- Batumi Botanical Garden: GEL 20/person (USD 7)
- Mtatsminda ropeway (Tbilisi): GEL 12/person return
- Mtskheta half-day with driver: USD 45–60
