## Why Seasonality Matters for GCC Travelers

Georgia's climate is dramatically different from the Arabian Peninsula. With four distinct seasons, the timing of your trip shapes everything from clothing needs to activity availability.

## Spring: March–May ★★★★★ Recommended

Spring is Georgia's most rewarding season for Gulf visitors. Temperatures in Tbilisi range from 12°C to 23°C. Mountain passes to Kazbegi open from late April. Countryside is lush with wildflowers, and hotels are less expensive than summer.

- **Ideal for:** Families, couples, first-time visitors.
- **Highlights:** Blossoming orchards in Kakheti wine region, Easter celebrations, Mtskheta pilgrimage routes.

## Summer: June–August ★★★★

July–August is Georgia's peak tourist season. Batumi is busy, accommodation prices rise 40–60%, and Kazbegi is crowded on weekends. However, Black Sea swimming is at its best.

- **Ideal for:** Beach-focused trips and Batumi city stays.
- **Tip:** Book accommodation 6–8 weeks in advance for July.

## Autumn: September–October ★★★★★ Recommended

September is the wine harvest (Rtveli) season in Kakheti. The Alazani Valley is stunning with amber foliage, and temperatures cool to a comfortable 20–26°C. Kazbegi mountain views are crystal clear.

- **Ideal for:** Couples, foodies, photography-focused trips.

## Winter: November–February ★★★

Ski season in Gudauri (2,196m) runs December–March. Tbilisi winters are cold (0–8°C) but manageable with proper clothing. Batumi is quiet and off-season rates are 40–50% lower.

- **Ideal for:** Budget travelers, ski families, and winter city breaks.
- **Note:** Kazbegi road conditions can be challenging; confirm with your driver before booking.

## Ramadan Planning

Georgia is a welcoming destination during Ramadan. Most Tbilisi restaurants accommodate late-night suhoor requests. Plan itinerary around prayer times and avoid scheduling long mountain drives during peak iftar windows.
