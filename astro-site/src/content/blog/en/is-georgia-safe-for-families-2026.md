## Overview: Georgia's Safety Record

Georgia is ranked as one of the safest tourist destinations in the wider Caucasus and Eastern European region. The Global Peace Index consistently places Georgia in the top 60–70 countries worldwide — safer than many Western European destinations popular with Gulf tourists.

Violent crime against tourists is extremely rare. Petty theft (pickpockets at Tbilisi's Dry Bridge Market and Batumi's crowded waterfront) is the primary concern.

## For Women Travelers

GCC women traveling in groups — or female-only groups — report very positive safety experiences in Georgia. Cultural norms favor conservative dress in historic areas and churches, which aligns naturally with modest clothing worn by Gulf visitors.

**Practical tips for women:**
- Old Town Tbilisi and Batumi Boulevard are safe after dark with normal vigilance.
- Dress modestly (covered shoulders/knees) when entering Georgian Orthodox churches.
- Ridesharing apps (Bolt, Yandex) are widely used and safer than unmarked taxis.

## Child-Friendly Considerations

- Tbilisi's Mtatsminda Park has a full amusement park suitable for ages 4–14.
- Batumi Boulevard is pedestrianized along 7km of seafront — ideal for strollers and young children.
- Kazbegi requires stamina for mountain hikes; lighter walks are available for families with small children.
