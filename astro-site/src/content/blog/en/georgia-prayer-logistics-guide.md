## Prayer Infrastructure in Georgia

Georgia has a Muslim minority, particularly in the Adjara and Kvemo Kartli regions. Tbilisi's Old Town has one of the Caucasus's oldest mosques, shared by both Sunni and Shia communities.

## Key Prayer Facilities by City

### Tbilisi

**Jumah Mosque (Tbilisi Mosque)**  
Location: Botanikuri Street 2, Old Town  
Open: Daily — all five prayers observed  
Wudu facilities: Available  
Note: This mosque serves both Sunni and Shia congregations — all are welcome.

**Prayer rooms:** Several hotels in Vake and Saburtalo districts provide prayer mats and Qibla compass direction cards on request. Ask at check-in.

### Batumi

**Batumi Mosque**  
Location: Baratashvili Street, city centre  
Open: Daily for all prayers  
Wudu facilities: Available  
The mosque serves the Adjarian Muslim community and is welcoming to visiting Muslims.

### Kazbegi / Stepantsminda

No mosque is present. The mountain town has a small Muslim prayer space near the guesthouse district. Your driver can identify the nearest prayer space. The open mountain landscape provides peaceful alternatives for individual prayer.

### Mtskheta, Kakheti, and rural areas

These are predominantly Orthodox Christian areas. Plan for individual prayer with a portable mat. The scenery makes for a deeply moving prayer experience.

## Prayer Time Notes

Georgia uses **UTC+4 (Georgian Standard Time)** year-round — no daylight saving change. This is:
- Same as Gulf Standard Time (UAE, Oman)
- +1 hour ahead of Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Qatar

**For Riyadh travelers:** Georgian Dhuhr falls approximately at Saudi Asr time. Plan city tours around the shift.

## Itinerary Planning Tips

1. Schedule Jumu'ah (Friday prayer) in Tbilisi Old Town or Batumi — both are logistically easy.
2. Build 20–25 minute prayer windows into mountain day trips.
3. Use apps: **Muslim Pro**, **Athan**, or **Qibla Connect** — all accurate for Georgia.
4. Your Georgia Hills driver is familiar with the prayer timetable and can adjust stops accordingly.
