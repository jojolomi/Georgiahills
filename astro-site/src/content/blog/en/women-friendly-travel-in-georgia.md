## Georgia for GCC Women: Overview

Georgia is an exceptionally welcoming destination for Gulf women, whether traveling solo, in pairs, or as organized groups. The country's low crime rate, visible tourist police presence, and cultural respect for modest dress make it a natural fit.

## Recommended Neighborhoods for Women Travelers

### Tbilisi
- **Vake and Saburtalo**: Modern, well-lit residential areas with excellent restaurants and easy access to malls and pharmacies.
- **Old Town (Abanotubani)**: Very safe for evening walks; crowded and lively until midnight in tourist season.
- Avoid poorly-lit industrial areas east of Didi Digomi after 23:00.

### Batumi
- **Boulevard area**: Tbilisi's equivalent of a seafront promenade — busy and well-policed.
- **Orbi City complex**: Large mixed-use complex with halal dining, shopping, and entertainment.

## Transport Safety

- **Private transfers (Georgia Hills)**: Safest and most convenient option for groups.
- **Bolt / Yandex Taxi**: Reputable apps with driver details visible before entry. Share your ride status with the group.
- **Metro**: Safe during daylight hours; less suitable at night for women traveling alone with luggage.

## Dress and Cultural Considerations

Georgia is secular and tolerant. Modest dress is respected and appreciated — no negative attention for headscarves or abayas. In fact, many Georgian women wear similar modest styles.

Church visits require covered shoulders and knees. Scarves are provided at major church entrances if needed.

## Women-Focused Experiences

- **Tbilisi Sulfur Baths (Chreli Abano)**: Private room rental available — no mixed bathing required. Excellent for groups.
- **Kakheti Wine Tours**: Private winery visits can be arranged for women-only groups with non-alcoholic Georgian juice options.
- **Tbilisi National Museum**: Excellent exhibits, accessible, and very female-friendly staff.
- **Batumi Botanical Garden**: Wide paths, scenic views, ideal for relaxed group walks.
