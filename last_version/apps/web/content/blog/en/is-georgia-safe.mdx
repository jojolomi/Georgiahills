# Is Georgia Safe for Family Travel?

> **Verified fact:** Georgia ranked 61st on the Global Peace Index 2024, one of the most stable countries in the Caucasus/Eastern Europe region for tourism. ([Source: Vision of Humanity GPI 2024](https://www.visionofhumanity.org/maps/#/))

The short answer: **yes, Georgia is generally safe** for GCC families. Tourist areas in Tbilisi, Batumi, and Kazbegi are well-established and cater to international visitors. Like any travel, the best experience comes from organized planning — not luck.

## Key Safety Areas to Consider

### 1. Transport and Mountain Routes

The road from Tbilisi to Kazbegi is scenic but mountainous. Always:
- Start mountain routes before 8:00 AM.
- Monitor weather forecasts — conditions change quickly in the Caucasus.
- Use a driver familiar with the route, not a random daily hire.

> **Expert note:** "The Jvari Pass on the Kazbegi route reaches 2,379m altitude. Snow can appear in spring and autumn. Our drivers monitor road conditions daily and will suggest alternative plans if needed." — Georgia Hills Team

### 2. Accommodation Safety

- Choose hotels with 24-hour reception.
- Read recent reviews on Booking.com or Google before finalizing.
- Select locations close to central areas, not isolated districts.

### 3. Daily Urban Safety

Tbilisi, Batumi, and other tourist areas have low crime rates against tourists. Common-sense precautions:
- Keep phone and valuables in a zipped bag.
- Use verified transport apps (Yandex Go, Bolt) or pre-booked private transfer.
- Avoid empty side streets late at night.

### 4. Food Safety

Stick to mid-range or well-reviewed restaurants. Street food is generally safe in the main tourist areas. If you have dietary requirements (halal, vegetarian), confirm with the restaurant before ordering.

## Pre-Trip Safety Checklist

- [ ] Digital copies of passports stored in cloud storage.
- [ ] Hotel address and phone saved in phone.
- [ ] Emergency contacts (local + home) saved.
- [ ] Local SIM or verified roaming activated.
- [ ] Basic first aid items and prescription medication packed.
- [ ] Driver contact confirmed for arrival.

## Women Traveling in Groups

Georgia is generally considered safe for women traveling together. Islamic dress is respected and the culture is hospitable. Pre-arranged private transport and confirmed accommodation remove most day-to-day uncertainty.

## Conclusion

Georgia is a family-safe destination when trips are organized. The combination of reliable private transport, good accommodation choices, and an early-start approach to mountain routes covers the vast majority of safety considerations. Most visitors return from Georgia with nothing but positive memories.
