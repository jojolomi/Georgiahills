Overview

Tbilisi, the beating heart of the Caucasus, is a city of contrasts where ancient sulphur baths sit beneath futuristic glass bridges. From the winding streets of the Old Town to the lively Rustaveli Avenue, Tbilisi offers a unique blend of history, culture, and famous Georgian hospitality. Whether you're a foodie, history buff, or nature lover, this city has something magical for you.

Top Highlights

  • Old Town (Alt-Tbilisi): Maze-like cobblestone streets and wooden balconies.
  • Narikala Fortress: Ancient 4th-century fortress overlooking the city.
  • Sulphur Baths (Abanotubani): Historic domed bathhouses essential for relaxation.
  • Sameba Cathedral: The massive Holy Trinity Cathedral, a symbol of modern Georgia.

Suggested 3-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Ancient & Modern

Start at Metekhi Church, take the cable car to Narikala, walk down to the Sulphur Baths, and end with a sunset walk across the Peace Bridge.

Day 2: City Culture

Stroll down Rustaveli Avenue, visit the National Museum, explore the Dry Bridge Market for antiques, and enjoy a traditional Supra dinner.

Day 3: Nature Escape

Take a trip to Mtatsminda Park via funicular for panoramic views, or visit the nearby Chronicle of Georgia monument.

Best Time to Visit

Spring & Autumn

(May-June, Sept-Oct) Perfect weather, blooming nature, and wine harvest festivals.

Winter

(Dec-Feb) Magical snowy atmosphere, fewer tourists, and cozy sulphur baths.

Common Questions

Is Tbilisi safe for tourists?

Yes, Tbilisi is one of the safest capitals in Europe. Locals are notoriously hospitable.

Do people speak English?

Younger generations speak excellent English. Older generations often speak Russian. Georgian is the native language.
